  • Q:  I want to come for the three days, but I’d rather do other things during the day and meet up with everybody each evening for the dinners in the parks. What is there to do around the area?
    A: 

    Great question! And I’m so glad you asked. The area is a great area for outdoorsmen/women. Here is a list of activities you can do as a day trip (if you are interested in any of these, you should google to get more information):

    • Pilot Butte and Wild Horse scenic loop. I don’t always see the wild horses on this loop, but more often than not, the herd is nearby which is fascinating. You can hike and climb Pilot Butte if you are so inclined.
    • For most of you coming from the west (Utah) toward Wyoming, you can take the Bridger Valley Historical byway on your way to the reunion. It is a 20 mile loop off of I-80 where all the historical trails met. The crossroads of the California/Oregon Trail, the Mormon trail, the Pony Express Route, the Transcontinental railroad and the Lincoln Highway all met here. If you have some extra time on your way to the reunion, take the loop to see these historical sites. You take Exit 34 off of I-80 and reconvenes at Exit 48.
    • Three things you can do in one day (if you hurry) or just spend time at one of these. The White Mountain Petroglyphs, Boar’s Tusk, and the Sand Dunes. About 10 miles north of Rock Springs off of Highway 191, you will turn off for about 2 miles on dirt roads to get to the Petroglyphs. You can head to the Boar’s Tusk and even hike and climb to the top if you’d like, or just spend the day in the Killpecker Sand Dunes. You can rent (if you don’t own) sleds and/or sandboards and hit the slopes of the sand dunes. Dune buggies, dirt bikes, and ATV’s make there way from across the county to hit this popular spot.
    • A short distance to the north is South Pass, which is another historic pioneer site. There you will encounter Rocky Ridge, one of the gloomiest times for the Martin and Willie Handcart companies. South Pass has a visitor’s center that you can check out. And on your way back to Rock Springs/Green River, you can stop at the Farson merc to get some ice cream like no other. Be careful though, if you order anything bigger than the baby size. You better have an appetite.
    • Rent a bike or bring your own mountain bike to explore some of the best trails. The Wilkins Peak Trail System allows mountain bikers of all levels to experience a unique landscape and challenging ride. Or just take to the lower elevation trails along the Green River.
    • Rent a boat to explore the Flaming Gorge by water. This is a wonderful activity for a family day outing.
    • When the group goes to float the Green River, we may have time to do the Sheep Creek Geological Loop, which has some great views and geological wonders. If not, you may choose to do this on your own. Just south of Manila Utah.
    • The Western Wyoming Community College in Rock Springs has dinosaur exhibits throughout the campus along with a Wildlife Museum that is fun for all ages.
    • Spend the day fishing along the Green or other areas. There is endless potential to explore and to create your ultimate fishing experience.
  • Q:  So what is the food situation? Will I be expected to provide meals for my family/group for the entire reunion?
    A: 

    We are attempting to make this as convenient as possible for everyone. And each day is a little different. On Day 1 with Martin’s Cove, you will need to provide for your own breakfast and pack a lunch that we will eat on the Trail. Dinner will then be provided that evening. Each family (of the 6 family groups) will have a meal assignment. The first assignment will be Thursday evening in a park in Rock Springs. Let me know in advance, and that family can have access to a mobile BBQ trailer, or you can do your own thing. Maybe a Dutch John dinner or whatever your heart desires. It is up to each family to plan for the evening meals. Assignments have not yet been made, but we will make those assignments soon. Day 2 at the Gorge, you will need to provide for your own breakfast only. Lunch will be provided by the reunion with hamburgers and hot dogs. That evening, we will be having dinner in another park in Green River. The mobile BBQ trailer will be available if the family would like. Day 3 will be at Expedition Island and a family will be assigned to breakfast and a family will be assigned to lunch. The Pavilion building that we reserved has a commercial kitchen that can be utilized for these two meals.

  • Q:  I want to float the river, but I am afraid of the rapids. Is this a calm float?
    A: 

    It is a very calm float. And is very relaxing. But there are a couple of areas of white water that you should be careful with. Everyone will be required to wear a life jacket by law. I have floated the river many times, and I would put me as a very cautious person. I did hold on tight in the small areas of rapids, but it was very fun. The views of the float are breathtaking, and I highly recommend you to float the river.
